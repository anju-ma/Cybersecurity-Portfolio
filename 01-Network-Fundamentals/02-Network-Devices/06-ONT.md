# Optical Network Terminal (ONT)

## What is an ONT?

An **Optical Network Terminal (ONT)** is a networking device that connects a home or office to a **fiber optic Internet connection**. It converts optical (light) signals from the ISP into digital electrical signals that a router can use, and converts outgoing digital data back into light signals.

---

## Why Do We Need an ONT?

Fiber optic cables transmit data using **light signals**, while routers use **digital electrical data**.

The ONT converts between these two forms, allowing communication between the router and the ISP.

---

## How Does an ONT Work?

1. A device sends data to the router.
2. The router forwards the data to the ONT.
3. The ONT converts the electrical data into light signals.
4. The light signals travel through the fiber optic cable to the ISP.
5. Incoming light signals are converted back into electrical data and sent to the router.

## Characteristics

- Used with fiber optic Internet connections.
- Converts light signals into electrical signals.
- Connects the customer to the ISP's fiber network.
- Installed at the customer's location.

---

## ONT vs Modem

| ONT | Modem |
|------|--------|
| Used for fiber Internet | Used for DSL or cable Internet |
| Converts light signals | Converts signals used by cable/DSL technologies |
| Connects to a fiber optic cable | Connects to cable or telephone lines |

---

## Cybersecurity Relevance

- Forms part of the network edge.
- Usually managed by the ISP.
- Should have updated firmware if user-manageable.
- Understanding ONTs helps security professionals understand fiber-based network connectivity.

---

## Key Takeaways

- An ONT is used with fiber optic Internet.
- It converts light signals into digital electrical data and vice versa.
- It performs a role similar to a modem but is designed specifically for fiber networks.
